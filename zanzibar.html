About Zanzibar

Zanzibar is a stunning archipelago located off the coast of Tanzania. Known for its crystal-clear waters, white sandy beaches, and vibrant culture, it's a dream destination for travelers seeking both relaxation and adventure. The island’s rich history blends African, Arab, Indian, and European influences.

Top Attractions

Nungwi Beach

Crystal-clear waters and breathtaking sunsets await you in Nungwi.

Stone Town

A UNESCO World Heritage site full of culture, history, and charm.

Prison Island

Home to giant tortoises and a historic quarantine station.

Cultural & Food

Zanzibar’s culture is a vibrant mix of Swahili, Arab, Indian, and European influences. Traditional music, dances, and festivals like *Sauti za Busara* bring the island to life.

The local cuisine includes spicy pilau, seafood dishes, and exotic fruits. Visit Forodhani Market for a taste of real Zanzibari flavor.

Travel Information

How to Get There

Zanzibar is accessible via ferry from Dar es Salaam or by flight directly to Abeid Amani Karume International Airport.

Best Time to Visit

June to October offers dry and sunny weather, perfect for beach holidays. December is also lively due to festivals.

Local Tips

Respect local culture, dress modestly in Stone Town, and always agree on prices before taking a taxi or boat ride.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a visa to visit Zanzibar?

Yes, most travelers need a visa to enter Tanzania. It can be obtained online or at the airport upon arrival.

Is Zanzibar safe for tourists?

Zanzibar is generally very safe. Like any tourist destination, it’s advised to stay aware and respect local customs.

What language is spoken in Zanzibar?

The official language is Swahili, but English is widely spoken in hotels, tours, and major tourist areas.

When is the best time to visit Zanzibar?

June to October is the dry season and ideal for beach holidays. December is also popular due to festive events.

Can I use credit cards in Zanzibar?

Credit cards are accepted in major hotels and restaurants, but cash (Tanzanian Shillings or USD) is preferred in local markets.
